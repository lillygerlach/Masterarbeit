# Master Thesis

## The Role of Overconfidence in Entrepreneurial Exit Decisions: Linking Two Distinct Streams of Literature
**Author:** Lilly Gerlach  
**Degree:** Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Business Administration  
**University:** Humboldt University of Berlin  
**Submission Date:** March 2026  


## Overview
This thesis examines how overconfidence influences entrepreneurial exit decisions under uncertainty. It bridges two major research streams: entrepreneurial exit and behavioral biases, specifically overconfidence.  
The study develops a behavioral real options perspective and integrates it with the decision-not-to-decide framework (Schade & Snir, 2020) to explain why entrepreneurs often delay exit decisions even under persistent underperformance. 


## Research Objective
The central research question is:  
How does overconfidence influence entrepreneurial exit timing and exit mode choice under uncertainty?  
The thesis analyzes how different forms of overconfidence affect:  
* Exit timing (when entrepreneurs exit)
* Exit mode choice (how they exit, e.g., sale, closure)
* Persistence and exit delay under uncertainty
